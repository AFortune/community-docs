Bite! Clojure

Welcome! This web site has a lot of (should be in near future) documents for the ClojureBridge event. The documents will be used as a supportive material. ClojureBridge has a main stream document (on going now); however, only one document would be unable to fit/satisfy all attendees of the events. Some might be programmers of other languages, some may be designers, or others are, probably, beginners of programming. For attendees of various backgounds, providing extra labs and explanations is a purpose of the documents here. So that everybody can cheerfully "Bite! Clojure".

The documents assume that the attendees will try lab problems with helps of coaches. Each document has advices to the coaches also(should be there). If you will volunteer as a coach, have a look at the advices to know what the attendees should learn.

Help Wanted!! GIVE US PULL REQUEST!
Yes, we really want helps from Clojure community. It is very hard for one or two people to write documents to cover a computer language. Gratefully, at the unsession of Clojure/Conj, also, ClojureBridge mailing list, we have gotten so many ideas and voices of "I can help". What an awesome community! If we can get your help to grow the documents here, that will be SUPER AWESOME!!
Fork the repo, write a document, and give us a pull request! We will merge your document, which will be a material at ClojureBridge events. How to Contribute guides you how to write.
